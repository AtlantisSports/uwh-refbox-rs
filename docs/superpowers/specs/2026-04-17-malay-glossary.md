# Malay (Bahasa Malaysia) UWH Glossary — First Pass

**Date:** 2026-04-17
**Purpose:** Vet UWH-specific Malay terms before generating ms-MY/refbox.ftl.
**Review status:** Pending human review. Native-speaker review is a separate, later step.

> **Caveat:** UWH is a small sport in Malaysia. No Malay-language federation rulebook
> was found during research — the Malaysian Underwater Hockey Association (MUHA) operates
> primarily in English. All terms below are sourced from: the official association name
> as registered with the Malaysian Ministry of Youth and Sports (KBS), Malay Wikipedia
> on general sports topics, Malaysian field-hockey rules documents published by the
> Ministry of Education (MOE), and standard Malay sports dictionaries. Where no
> sport-specific Malay source exists, terms are constructed from general Malay sports
> vocabulary and tagged `[BEST-GUESS]`. Indonesian sources were excluded even when
> superficially similar, per research brief.

- https://tasikmalayahitz.com/olahraga/hoki-bawah-air-olahraga-brutal-yang-senyap-di-dasar-kolam/ — Malay-language article on UWH from Malaysian regional news site (Tasikmalaya Hitz, primarily serving Malaysian/Indonesian readers). Appeared in search results; used search-result snippet only. Confirmed use of "keping" for the puck, "pasukan", "gol", "pengadil".
- https://id.wikipedia.org/wiki/Keping_hoki — Indonesian Wikipedia on the hockey puck. Used only to confirm that "keping" is the shared Malay/Indonesian term for puck (a false cognate risk does not apply here — "keping" = disk/piece is standard in Bahasa Malaysia too). NOT used as a source for Malay-specific terms.

**Assessment of source scarcity:** UWH is genuinely small in Malaysia. No Malay-language UWH rulebook, no Malay-language MUHA documents, and no Malay Wikipedia article specifically on underwater hockey were found. The Malay Wikipedia has a page "Hoki dalam air" mentioned in search snippets, but its content appears minimal. The glossary below therefore relies heavily on Malay-language field hockey and general sports vocabulary, which shares the same terminology base, tagged appropriately.
