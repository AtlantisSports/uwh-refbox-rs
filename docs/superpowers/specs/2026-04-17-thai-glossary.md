# Thai UWH Glossary — First Pass

**Date:** 2026-04-17
**Purpose:** Vet UWH-specific Thai terms before generating th-TH/refbox.ftl.
**Review status:** Pending human review. Native-speaker review is a separate, later step.

> **Important caveat:** Thai competitive underwater hockey is extremely new. The first formal
> training session in Thailand was held on 8 February 2025, run by ATUS (Association of Thailand
> Underwater Sports). No Thai-language UWH rulebook has been published. There is no Thai Wikipedia
> article for "ฮอกกี้ใต้น้ำ" (as of April 2026). The vast majority of terms below are drawn
> from established Thai sports vocabulary (water polo, ice hockey, handball, football) and tagged
> accordingly. A Thai-speaking UWH practitioner must review this list before the translation file
> is considered authoritative.

## Glossary (39 terms)

Trimmed to rows that correspond to concepts actually present in `en-US/refbox.ftl`.

| English (from en-US/refbox.ftl) | Thai | Source | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Black team | ทีมดำ | [BEST-GUESS] | Literal construction: ทีม (team) + ดำ (black/dark). Thai UWH sources describe the sport as having two teams distinguished by dark vs. light cap/stick colour but do not use a fixed label. "ทีมดำ" is the natural Thai parallel to "ทีมขาว". Scoreboard caps form: "ดำ". |
| White team | ทีมขาว | [BEST-GUESS] | Literal construction: ทีม (team) + ขาว (white/light). Parallel to ทีมดำ. Scoreboard caps form: "ขาว". |
| Score | คะแนน | [WIKIPEDIA-TH] | Standard Thai sports term for score/points. Used in water polo, football, and all team sports in Thai. Alternative: สกอร์ (transliteration of "score"). คะแนน is more formal; สกอร์ is common in broadcast. |
| Confirm (score) | ยืนยัน | [THAI-SPORTS] | Standard Thai verb meaning "confirm/verify." Used in sports contexts as "ยืนยันคะแนน" (confirm the score). Source: general Thai UI and sports vocabulary. |
| Foul (general) | ฟาวล์ | [WIKIPEDIA-TH] | Direct loanword, confirmed in Thai water polo (th.wikipedia.org/wiki/โปโลน้ำ) and futsal rules. Widely used across all Thai sports. |
| Warning (caution) | การเตือน | [THAI-SPORTS] | Confirmed in Thai futsal rules (kroobannok.com) and ice hockey context. "การเตือน" = an official caution. Also seen: "คำเตือน". Distinct from "ฟาวล์" (foul) — this is a formal disciplinary notice without time penalty. |
| Penalty (time-served) | โทษ | [WIKIPEDIA-TH] | Standard Thai sports term for a time penalty or punitive sanction. Confirmed in water polo (th.wikipedia.org/wiki/โปโลน้ำ) and futsal rules. Full phrase: "โทษพักการแข่งขัน" (penalty sit-out). |
| 30-second penalty | โทษ 30 วินาที | [BEST-GUESS] | Constructed from "โทษ" (penalty) + "30 วินาที" (30 seconds). No Thai UWH source uses this tier explicitly; the 30-second tier is uncommon in published Thai aquatic sports coverage. Scoreboard short form: "30วิ". |
| 1-minute penalty | โทษ 1 นาที | [THAI-SPORTS] | Constructed from "โทษ" + "1 นาที". Parallel to usage in water polo Thai sources where timed exclusions are described in minutes. Scoreboard short form: "1น." |
| 2-minute penalty | โทษ 2 นาที | [THAI-SPORTS] | Same construction. Most commonly referenced timed penalty in Thai aquatic sports contexts. Scoreboard short form: "2น." |
| 4-minute penalty | โทษ 4 นาที | [BEST-GUESS] | Constructed; 4-minute tier not referenced in Thai sports sources. Scoreboard short form: "4น." |
| 5-minute penalty | โทษ 5 นาที | [THAI-SPORTS] | Confirmed structure from water polo Thai sources where 5-minute exclusions appear. Scoreboard short form: "5น." |
| Total dismissal | ไล่ออก | [THAI-SPORTS] | Confirmed in Thai futsal/football sources as the term for permanent expulsion from the match (equivalent of red card expulsion). Full phrase: "ถูกไล่ออกจากสนาม" (dismissed from the field). In UWH context, without a card system, "ไล่ออก" captures the permanent removal concept. Scoreboard abbreviation: "ไล่" (best-guess short form). |
| First half | ครึ่งแรก | [WIKIPEDIA-TH] | Confirmed in Thai water polo Wikipedia and futsal rules. Literally "first half". Also seen: "ครึ่งที่หนึ่ง". |
| Second half | ครึ่งที่สอง | [WIKIPEDIA-TH] | Confirmed in Thai water polo Wikipedia and futsal rules. Literally "second half". |
| Half time | พักครึ่ง | [WIKIPEDIA-TH] | Confirmed in Thai water polo Wikipedia. Literally "rest at half". Standard across Thai team sports. |
| Between games (nominal break) | พักระหว่างเกม | [BEST-GUESS] | Constructed from "พัก" (rest/break) + "ระหว่าง" (between) + "เกม" (game). No Thai UWH source uses this UI phrase. Alternative: "ช่วงพักระหว่างเกม". |
| Overtime | ต่อเวลาพิเศษ | [WIKIPEDIA-TH] | Confirmed in Thai Wikipedia เวลาพิเศษ article and Thai water polo rules overview. Literally "extend special time". Also seen shortened to "ต่อเวลา" in Thai sports broadcasts. |
| Overtime first half | ครึ่งแรกต่อเวลา | [BEST-GUESS] | Constructed from ครึ่งแรก + ต่อเวลา. No Thai UWH source uses this phrase; overtime halves are not described in Thai aquatic sports coverage reviewed. |
| Overtime half time | พักครึ่งต่อเวลา | [BEST-GUESS] | Constructed from พักครึ่ง + ต่อเวลา. Same caveat as above. |
| Overtime second half | ครึ่งที่สองต่อเวลา | [BEST-GUESS] | Constructed. Same caveat. |
| Sudden death | ตายกะทันหัน | [BEST-GUESS] | Literal translation of "sudden death." The Thai word "กะทันหัน" means sudden/abrupt; "ตาย" means death/dead. This construction is not established in Thai aquatic sports; Thai Wikipedia's เวลาพิเศษ article describes the concept in prose ("การแข่งขันจนกว่าฝ่ายใดฝ่ายหนึ่งจะทำคะแนนได้ก่อน") without a fixed label. "ตายกะทันหัน" follows the same pattern as the English idiom and is comprehensible to Thai readers. A Thai UWH practitioner should confirm or replace this. |
| Team timeout | พักทีม | [THAI-SPORTS] | "ไทม์เอาต์" (transliteration of timeout) is used in Thai water polo and basketball contexts; "พักทีม" (team break) is the more natural Thai construction. Both are acceptable; "ไทม์เอาต์ทีม" is also seen. Source: Thai water polo rules on siamsporttalk.com. |
| Penalty shot | การยิงลูกโทษ | [WIKIPEDIA-TH] | Confirmed in Thai water polo Wikipedia ("การยิงจุดโทษ" in football; "การยิงลูกโทษ" in water polo). Both are understood. Short form on ribbon: "ยิงโทษ". |
| Referee (generic) | ผู้ตัดสิน | [WIKIPEDIA-TH] | Standard Thai sports term for referee/official. Confirmed in Thai water polo Wikipedia, ice hockey association, and futsal rules. Also "กรรมการ" (judge/official); "ผู้ตัดสิน" is slightly more formal and match-context specific. |
| Chief referee | หัวหน้าผู้ตัดสิน | [THAI-SPORTS] | Confirmed construction from Thai referees article (hmong.in.th/wiki/Referees). Literally "head referee." Used in Thai sports contexts for the senior officiating position. |
| Water referee | ผู้ตัดสินในน้ำ | [BEST-GUESS] | Constructed: ผู้ตัดสิน (referee) + ในน้ำ (in the water). No Thai UWH source uses this role title. In Thai water polo, officials are simply "ผู้ตัดสิน"; the "water" qualifier is specific to UWH. Alternative: "กรรมการใต้น้ำ". |
| Timekeeper | กรรมการจับเวลา | [THAI-SPORTS] | Confirmed in Thai water polo rules (siamsporttalk.com and hmong.in.th). Literally "official who keeps/holds time". Also: "ผู้จับเวลา". |
| Scorer (scorekeeping official) | กรรมการบันทึกคะแนน | [BEST-GUESS] | Constructed from กรรมการ (official) + บันทึก (record) + คะแนน (score). Thai water polo rules mention a "head scorekeeper" role without giving it a standard Thai-language label in the sources reviewed. |
| Cap number (player cap number) | หมายเลขหมวก | [BEST-GUESS] | Constructed: หมายเลข (number) + หมวก (cap/hat). UWH uses a protective cap; "หมวก" is the natural Thai word. Alternative: "เบอร์หมวก" (cap number, more colloquial). |
| Player | ผู้เล่น | [WEB-UWH-TH] | Confirmed in both the VOA Thai UWH article and the station-thai.com training coverage. Standard Thai sports term. |
| Stick foul | ฟาวล์ด้วยไม้ | [BEST-GUESS] | Constructed: ฟาวล์ (foul) + ด้วย (with/using) + ไม้ (stick/wood). Thai UWH training articles use "ไม้" for the UWH stick. No Thai source names this infraction category. |
| Illegal advance | การรุกผิดกติกา | [BEST-GUESS] | Constructed: การรุก (advance/attack) + ผิดกติกา (against the rules). No Thai sports source uses this exact label. Alternative: "การรุกล้ำผิดกติกา". |
| Sub foul (substitution foul) | ฟาวล์การเปลี่ยนตัว | [BEST-GUESS] | Constructed: ฟาวล์ (foul) + การเปลี่ยนตัว (substitution/player change). Standard Thai sports phrase "การเปลี่ยนตัวผู้เล่น" is common. No Thai UWH source names this category. |
| Illegal stoppage | การหยุดเล่นผิดกติกา | [BEST-GUESS] | Constructed: การหยุดเล่น (stopping play) + ผิดกติกา (against the rules). No Thai source uses this exact refbox category label. |
| Out of bounds | นอกสนาม | [THAI-SPORTS] | Standard Thai sports phrase used in football, basketball, and handball contexts: "ลูกออกนอกสนาม" (ball out of bounds). Confirmed across multiple Thai sports rule documents. Shortened: "นอกเขต". |
| Grabbing the wall | การจับผนัง | [BEST-GUESS] | Constructed: การจับ (grabbing/holding) + ผนัง (wall). Thai UWH articles describe the pool wall but no fixed Thai term exists for this infraction. Alternative: "จับขอบสระ" (grabbing the pool edge). |
| Obstruction | การขัดขวาง | [THAI-SPORTS] | Used in Thai handball and football contexts for obstruction/blocking. Confirmed in Thai futsal rules as "ขัดขวาง". Alternative from water polo context: "การขวางทาง". |
| Delay of game | การประวิงเวลา | [THAI-SPORTS] | Confirmed in Thai football/futsal context: "การประวิงเวลา" = intentionally wasting time. Standard Thai sports phrasing. |
| Unsportsmanlike conduct | การประพฤติผิดน้ำใจนักกีฬา | [THAI-SPORTS] | Confirmed in Thai futsal rules (kroobannok.com): "พฤติกรรมที่ไม่เป็นน้ำใจนักกีฬา" — literally "behaviour that lacks sportsmanship." Shortened: "ผิดน้ำใจนักกีฬา". |
| Free arm | แขนอิสระ | [BEST-GUESS] | Literal translation: แขน (arm) + อิสระ (free/independent). Thai UWH sources do not name this infraction category. This concept (use of the non-stick arm) is described in CMAS rules prose but has no established Thai label. |
| False start | การออกตัวผิด | [THAI-SPORTS] | Confirmed in Thai swimming and athletics contexts: "การออกตัวผิดกติกา" or "ออกตัวก่อนสัญญาณ" (leaving before the signal). Shortened form "การออกตัวผิด" is widely understood in Thai aquatic sports. |
